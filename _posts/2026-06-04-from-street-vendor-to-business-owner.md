At 68 years old, Lola Nena stands proudly in front of her thriving restaurant in Quezon City, a far cry from the street corner where she sold banana cue for decades. Her story is a testament to perseverance, hard work, and the Filipino spirit of never giving up.

## Humble Beginnings

Nena Reyes grew up in a small farming village in Batangas. After finishing only elementary school, she moved to Manila at age 18 to help support her family. With no formal education or connections, she started selling banana cue on the streets of Cubao.

### The Early Years

Life as a street vendor was challenging:

- Waking up at 3 AM to prepare her products
- Enduring harsh weather conditions
- Facing harassment from authorities
- Earning barely enough to survive

Despite these hardships, Lola Nena never complained. "Every day was a blessing," she recalls. "I was grateful to have something to sell and customers to serve."

## Building a Reputation

What set Lola Nena apart was her dedication to quality and customer service. She used only the freshest bananas, perfected her coating recipe, and always greeted customers with a warm smile.

### Word of Mouth

Her banana cue became legendary in the neighborhood:

> "People would come from other barangays just to buy from Lola Nena. Her banana cue was different – crunchier, sweeter, and made with love." - Regular customer since 1985

## The Turning Point

In 2015, at age 57, Lola Nena faced a crisis. The city government cracked down on street vendors, and she lost her regular spot. Many would have given up, but not Lola Nena.

### A Leap of Faith

With her life savings of ₱50,000 and encouragement from loyal customers, she decided to rent a small space and open a proper food stall. It was a huge risk, but she believed in her products and her customers.

## Growing the Business

The small food stall was an immediate success. Customers who had followed her for years were thrilled to have a permanent place to enjoy her food. But Lola Nena didn't stop at banana cue.

### Menu Expansion

She gradually added more items:

1. **Turon** - Her special recipe with jackfruit and banana
2. **Camote Cue** - Sweet potato fritters
3. **Maruya** - Banana fritters
4. **Halo-Halo** - A refreshing dessert
5. **Traditional Filipino Snacks** - Puto, kutsinta, and more

Each item was prepared with the same care and quality that made her banana cue famous.

## The Restaurant Dream

By 2020, Lola Nena had saved enough to expand into a full restaurant. She named it "Lola Nena's Kusina" and added rice meals to the menu, featuring traditional Filipino dishes.

### Family Involvement

Her success allowed her to bring her family into the business:

- Her daughter manages the finances
- Her son handles marketing and social media
- Her grandchildren help during weekends and school breaks

"This business is not just mine," Lola Nena says. "It belongs to my family and everyone who supported me along the way."

## Recognition and Success

Today, Lola Nena's Kusina has:

- Three branches across Metro Manila
- 25 employees
- Features in food blogs and magazines
- A loyal customer base spanning three generations

### Awards and Honors

Her inspiring story has earned recognition:

- **2024**: Outstanding Entrepreneur Award from the local government
- **2025**: Featured in a national TV documentary
- **2026**: Invited to speak at business seminars

## Giving Back

Despite her success, Lola Nena hasn't forgotten her roots. She regularly:

- Provides free meals to street vendors
- Mentors aspiring entrepreneurs
- Donates to her hometown in Batangas
- Offers jobs to people who need a second chance

### Scholarship Program

She established a scholarship fund for children of street vendors, helping them get the education she never had. "Education is the key to breaking the cycle of poverty," she emphasizes.

## Life Lessons from Lola Nena

When asked about her success, Lola Nena shares these principles:

### 1. Quality Over Quantity

"Never compromise on quality. Your reputation is built on every product you sell."

### 2. Treat Everyone with Respect

"Whether they buy one piece or ten, every customer deserves a smile and good service."

### 3. Save and Invest Wisely

"I saved every extra peso. Small amounts add up over time."

### 4. Never Stop Learning

"I may not have formal education, but I learn something new every day from my customers and employees."

### 5. Stay Humble

"Success can disappear as quickly as it came. Stay grounded and grateful."

## The Secret Ingredient

When pressed about what makes her food special, Lola Nena smiles and says:

> "The secret ingredient is love. I cook every dish as if I'm feeding my own family. That's something you can't fake or buy."

## Looking to the Future

At 68, Lola Nena shows no signs of slowing down. She's planning:

- A cookbook featuring her recipes
- Franchising opportunities for aspiring entrepreneurs
- A foundation to help street vendors transition to formal businesses
- Expansion to other cities

### Advice for Aspiring Entrepreneurs

"Don't wait for the perfect moment. Start where you are with what you have. Be patient, work hard, and never lose faith in yourself."

## A Legacy of Hope

Lola Nena's story resonates with millions of Filipinos who struggle daily to make ends meet. She proves that with determination, integrity, and hard work, it's possible to transform your life regardless of your starting point.

Her restaurant has become more than just a place to eat – it's a symbol of hope and possibility. Every customer who walks through the door is reminded that dreams can come true, no matter how impossible they may seem.

## The Heart of a Champion

Despite her success, Lola Nena still wakes up early every morning to oversee the preparation of food in her restaurants. She greets regular customers by name and remembers their favorite orders.

"This is not work for me," she says with a twinkle in her eye. "This is my passion, my purpose, and my way of serving others."
